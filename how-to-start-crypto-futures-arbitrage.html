🏦 Exchange Accounts — The Most Important Requirement

Crypto arbitrage means buying an asset on one exchange where the price is lower, and simultaneously selling it on another exchange where the price is higher. To do this, you must have active, funded accounts on at least two exchanges at the same time.

This is non-negotiable. You cannot do cross-exchange arbitrage with a single account. The moment you spot a price gap, both legs of the trade — the buy and the sell — need to execute almost simultaneously. If you only have one account, the gap will close before you can transfer funds.

MUST HAVE
🏦
Minimum 2 Exchange Accounts
One to buy (cheaper exchange), one to sell (expensive exchange). Both must be active and funded before a signal arrives.
MUST HAVE
✅
KYC Completed
Identity verification must be done in advance. KYC during a live trade is impossible. Most exchanges take 5–15 minutes to verify.
MUST HAVE
💵
Funds on Both Exchanges
USDT (or USDC) must already be sitting on both exchanges. You cannot transfer during a trade — gaps close in seconds to minutes.
RECOMMENDED
➕
3rd Exchange Account
A third exchange (like MEXC or Gate.io) multiplies your signal opportunities. Many ArbVertex signals use 3-exchange combinations.
⚠️
Common Beginner Mistake: Opening accounts but not completing KYC or not funding them in advance. When a signal arrives, you have minutes — not hours — to act. Setup must be done before you subscribe to signals.

💰 How Much Capital Do You Need?

The good news: you don't need a lot to start. Arbitrage percentage returns are the same regardless of capital size — the difference is only in absolute profit. $100 on each exchange is enough to start learning and executing real trades.

💵 Capital Scenarios — What to Expect
Capital Per Exchange Total Capital Profit on 2% Signal Best For
$100 $200 ~$2 Learning & practice
$500 $1,000 ~$10 Beginners — real results
$1,000 $2,000 ~$20 Serious traders
$5,000 $10,000 ~$100 Full-time arbitrage

Always keep funds split evenly across exchanges — if you have $1,000 total, put $500 on Binance and $500 on Bybit. Unequal balances mean you can't execute the full signal on both sides.

⚠️
Never trade with money you can't afford to lose. Arbitrage is lower risk than directional trading, but execution errors, slippage, and sudden market moves can still cause losses. Start small, learn the process, then scale.

📱 Devices — Why Two Screens Help

You can do arbitrage on a single device — but two devices makes execution significantly faster and less error-prone. When a signal arrives, you need to place orders on two exchanges almost simultaneously. Switching between tabs on one device costs precious seconds.

✅ RECOMMENDED SETUP
Two Devices
  • Exchange A open on Device 1
  • Exchange B open on Device 2
  • No tab switching — instant execution
  • Can monitor both order books at once
  • Reduces execution errors significantly
  • PC + Mobile is ideal combination
WORKS BUT SLOWER
Single Device
  • Both exchanges open in separate tabs
  • Alt+Tab or swipe between exchanges
  • Slight delay between both legs
  • Fine for slower arbitrage signals
  • Not ideal for tight time windows
  • Still profitable — just less efficient
💡
Best device combination: PC with Binance open + Mobile with Bybit app open. This way you have full keyboard control on one exchange and instant mobile access on the other. Most ArbVertex traders use exactly this setup.

🌐 Internet Connection

Arbitrage does not require ultra-fast internet like HFT (high-frequency trading). A normal 4G mobile connection or home broadband is perfectly fine. What matters more is stability — a connection that drops mid-trade can leave you with an open position on one exchange and nothing on the other.

MINIMUM
📶
Stable 4G / Broadband
Any consistent connection works. Speed matters less than reliability — avoid trading on weak or switching connections.
RECOMMENDED
⚡
Backup Connection Ready
Keep mobile data as backup if your WiFi drops mid-trade. Switching to hotspot takes seconds and can save an open position.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many exchange accounts do I need for crypto arbitrage?
A minimum of 2 exchange accounts — one to buy (cheaper exchange) and one to sell (more expensive exchange). Start with Binance + Bybit. Adding MEXC or Gate.io as a third exchange significantly increases the number of signals you can act on.
How much money do I need to start crypto arbitrage?
You can start with as little as $100 per exchange ($200 total). The percentage return on each trade is the same regardless of capital size — only the absolute profit changes. Most beginners start with $500 per exchange for meaningful results.
Do I need two devices for crypto arbitrage?
Two devices are strongly recommended but not required. With two devices, you can have one exchange open on each screen and execute both legs of the trade simultaneously. A single device with two tabs works, but is slightly slower. Most traders use PC + mobile.
Can I do crypto arbitrage on mobile only?
Yes — all exchanges have mobile apps. Two mobiles is a viable setup: open Binance on one phone and Bybit on the other. This is actually a popular setup for traders who are always on the move. Signal notifications come via Telegram on the same phone.
Do I need to complete KYC before trading?
Yes — complete KYC in advance. Most exchanges require KYC for full withdrawal access. The process takes 5–15 minutes but may require up to 24 hours for approval on some platforms. Never wait for a signal to arrive before starting KYC.
Which currency should I fund my exchange accounts with?
USDT (Tether) is the standard. Fund both exchanges with USDT — it's the most liquid stablecoin and is used in 90%+ of arbitrage pairs. Use the TRC-20 (Tron) network for transfers between exchanges — it's fastest and cheapest (usually under $1 fee).
