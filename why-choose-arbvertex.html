What "Human-Verified" Actually Means

Automated scanners detect price gaps across exchanges — but they have no judgment. They will alert you to a 5% spread on a coin being delisted in 6 hours (where withdrawal is blocked), or a gap caused by a single illiquid order that will close the moment you try to fill it.

Every ArbVertex signal goes through a manual check before it is sent:

🔍

Delisting check

Coins flagged for delisting on either exchange are immediately excluded — these look like arbitrage but the spread often cannot be safely closed.

💧

Liquidity validation

Thin order books create gaps that vanish on execution. Signals are only sent when enough liquidity exists to fill a real position at the quoted spread.

🧮

Net fee calculation

Taker fees, withdrawal fees, and typical slippage are subtracted before a signal is sent. The 2%+ figure shown is what you can realistically capture — not the raw spread.

⚡

Speed filtering

Gaps that close in under 30 seconds are excluded. Signals are only sent when execution windows are wide enough for manual traders — not just bots.

Quality Over Quantity

"Sending 50 signals a day that average 0.8% net is worse than sending 3 signals that average 2.5% net. Fewer, better opportunities protect your capital and your time."

Most subscribers report that 2–3 well-chosen signals per day is optimal for manual execution. More signals means more execution risk, more fees, and more cognitive load. ArbVertex is deliberately designed to fit around a normal schedule — not require full-time monitoring.
